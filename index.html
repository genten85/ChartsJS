Reported fatality rate of COVID-19 by country

This data is approximate and from March 2, 2020

The fatality rate of COVID-19 is determined based on the number of deaths over number of cases. While the number of cases in the United States is low relative to other countries, there have been a relatively great number of reported deaths. It can be suggested countries with lower rates has greater cases but less deaths, which suggests in those countries, the disease is less deadly. It is however important to stress that the cases in these countries are not good estimates because each country has different methodologies in testing and confirming cases. In addition, there are people with mild cases who might have been turned away and unreported or people who have yet to seek medical attention.
